Altair Ibn La Ahad

Altaïr Ibn-La'Ahad was a member of the Levantine Brotherhood of Assassins who served as their Mentor from 1191 until his death in 1257. During his tenure as Mentor, through the knowledge of an Apple of Eden, Altaïr made several discoveries and inventions that greatly helped the Order's progression.

Timeline

  • 1165: Altaïr is born in Syria to Assassin parents, Maud and Umar Ibn-La'Ahad.
  • 1176: Altaïr's father, Umar, is executed by the Saracens during the Siege of Masyaf.
  • Before 1191: Altaïr becomes a Master Assassin, the youngest in the Order's history.
  • 1191: Altaïr fails to retrieve an Apple of Eden from Robert de Sablé, leading to the Templars attacking Masyaf.
  • 1191 (July-September): Altaïr is demoted and tasked with assassinating nine individuals, who are revealed to be Templars.
  • 1191 (Post-demotion): Altaïr embarks on a quest to redeem himself and liberate the Holy Land from Templar influence.
  • 1190 (One year prior to AC1): Assassin's Creed: Altaïr's Chronicles takes place, focusing on Altaïr's search for the Chalice and his conflict with the Templars.
  • 1257: Altaïr dies, leaving behind a legacy of discoveries and inventions that shaped the Assassin Order's future.
